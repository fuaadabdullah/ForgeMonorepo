## Knowledge Management Guidelines

### For Knowledge Owners

1. **Keep it Current**: Regularly review and update knowledge as systems evolve
2. **Make it Findable**: Use clear titles, tags, and cross-references
3. **Ensure Quality**: Include context, examples, and validation steps
4. **Share Widely**: Make knowledge accessible to all who need it
5. **Track Impact**: Monitor how knowledge is used and improved

### For Knowledge Consumers

1. **Verify Context**: Ensure the knowledge applies to your situation
2. **Test Assumptions**: Validate that recommendations work in your environment
3. **Provide Feedback**: Report gaps or improvements needed
4. **Contribute Back**: Share your experiences and lessons learned
5. **Stay Updated**: Check for updates to critical knowledge

### Quality Standards

- **Completeness**: All sections filled with meaningful content
- **Accuracy**: Information verified through testing or multiple sources
- **Clarity**: Written in clear, understandable language
- **Actionability**: Provides specific steps and recommendations
- **Maintainability**: Includes review cycles and update triggers

*This template ensures institutional knowledge is captured, validated, and maintained for long-term organizational learning.*
